Metrics on the Predict page

StockSensei pulls recent prices from Yahoo Finance and turns them into model features. These metrics describe what recently happened; they do not guarantee what happens next.

OpenThe first traded price of the day. It can gap up or down from yesterday's close.
High / LowThe day's trading range. A wide range can signal uncertainty or volatility.
Daily ReturnThe percent change from one close to the next close.
VolumeThe number of shares traded. Bigger moves with bigger volume can matter more.
5-Day Moving AverageA short trend line that reacts quickly to recent price changes.
10-Day Moving AverageA smoother short-term trend line. Compare it with the 5-day average.
VolatilityHow much the stock has been moving around recently.
ConfidenceHow strongly the model chose its output. Low confidence should be treated carefully.

What “Prediction” really means

The model is making a probability-style estimate from recent data. A result of Up means the model sees recent patterns that look more like past upward examples. Down means the opposite. Uncertain means confidence is too low to present it strongly.

Prediction = recent features → trained model → Up / Down / Uncertain

For the MVP, the prediction is best used as a learning signal. Better accuracy would require testing the model on unseen historical periods, comparing it against a baseline, and retraining if performance is weak.

How to think about risk

Risk level combines model confidence with recent volatility. A high-confidence prediction on a calm stock is different from a low-confidence prediction on a stock swinging wildly.

1.Check whether the confidence is high enough to care about.
2.Check volatility. Bigger recent swings mean the outcome can change quickly.
3.Check the chart. A prediction without trend context is incomplete.
4.Check news or earnings before making any real-world decision.
